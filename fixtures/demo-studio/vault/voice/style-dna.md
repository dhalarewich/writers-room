# Style DNA — Field Notes

Distilled from the pieces in [[samples]]. Synthetic demo voice: use it to see how the studio behaves, then replace with your own via /setup.

## 1. Sentence rhythm

Opens short. A scene or a number before any opinion. Middle paragraphs run longer, usually one concrete observation unpacked, then a short close that lands the cost of the finding. Single-sentence paragraphs are reserved for the one thing the reader should repeat to a friend.

## 2. Vocabulary

Reaches for: field verbs (delaminated, wicked, glassed over), exact quantities (34 nights, 210 grams, second season), brand names when earned.
Avoids: catalog adjectives (premium, rugged, bombproof), superlatives without a test behind them, the word "gamechanger" in any form.

## 3. Hook patterns

- The failure, stated flat: "The zipper died on night nine."
- The number that shouldn't be true: "This stove costs four dollars a gram."
- The confession: "I packed the wrong shelter for a decade."
Never opens with the conclusion; earns it by the close.

## 4. Transitions

"But here's the part the spec sheet hides." Pivots on the tested detail, not on rhetoric. Callbacks to the opening scene at the close.

## 5. Opinion stances

Weight matters less than sleep quality. Cottage brands earn trust by repair policy, not by fabric names. A review without a failure report is an ad.

## 6. Characteristic moves

Test log → what broke → what that costs you at 2am → who should still buy it. Ends by naming who the gear is NOT for.

## 7. Never does

No affiliate-link hedging. No "your mileage may vary" cop-outs. No spec-sheet recitals without a night outside behind them.

## 8. Known gaps

Winter gear underrepresented; only two seasons of ski touring in the log.
