# Rubric

The Critic scores every idea against these criteria; the weighted total goes to the card's `score` field. Below 40 earns a `kill` tag, 85 and above earns `priority`.

Calibration: a 70 means "publishable with work." An 85 means "drop everything and write this." A 95 should be rare — if you give one, you're vouching for it. A wall of 7s makes the rubric useless; if your scores cluster, recalibrate against three recent highs and three recent lows.

## Specificity (weight: 30)

Does the idea name actual people, exact numbers, real moments, named companies, specific decisions — or is it gesturing at "many companies" and "the AI era"? Specific beats general every time. A generic idea cannot survive drafting in a real voice: if specificity is missing at idea stage, it will be missing in the draft.

## Strategic value (weight: 25)

Does this ladder to the north star in [[strategy]]? Would it still be worth writing if it got no engagement?

## Audience fit (weight: 20)

Which audience is this for, and how cleanly does it land for them? A clean single-audience piece outscores a muddled two-audience piece.

## Novelty (weight: 15)

Deliberately low weight: the operator doesn't need brand-new topics, they need true things from their specific vantage. Score high only for genuinely fresh ground; note explicitly when a take treads ground worn smooth.

## Urgency (weight: 10)

Tied to a passing moment? Used to jump the queue, not to penalize evergreens.
